Why solar deals die, and how efficiency-first revives them.

Every stalled proposal dies of the same three things. Each one is a function of system size, and system size is a function of load.

  • The objection that kills it What efficiency-first does
  • “The upfront price is too high.” Sizing to the raw bill inflates the system. The sticker shock kills the deal before payback ever enters the conversation. Cut 20–30% of the load and the array, and its price, shrinks by the same share.
  • “The payback takes too long.” On a big system the ROI horizon stretches past what the owner will commit to, so the proposal sits. Efficiency savings start day one and help fund the array, pulling payback in by months.
  • “We don't have the roof / budget for that.” An oversized array runs out of roof or capital. A 'no' on size reads as a 'no' on solar entirely. A smaller system fits the roof and the budget, turning a hard no into a staged yes.

A third of the building's load is waste. So why are you sizing the array to cover it?

~30 %

The EPA's figure for the average commercial building. Quote solar against the raw bill and a quarter of the system you sell exists only to power waste, raising the price and the payback you have to defend.
